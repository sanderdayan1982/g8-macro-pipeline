MDP (Monetary Disorder Premium) = price↔fair-value misalignment under the MMT/Mosler lens: gold as a thermometer of sovereign-liability disorder, not as an opportunity cost. Drivers: breakeven (purchasing power of the liability) · slope 10Y−EFFR (observable term premium, free of any ACM model) · log NFA_total = FDHBFIN+WRESBAL−WTREGEN (net financial assets of the non-government sector, Mosler metric). DXY enters with a fixed beta outside the TVP. The equilibrium MOVES: betas evolve with the fiscal stance (β_t = β_{t-1} + η_t, the forgetting factor δ constrains the drift). Look-ahead-free signal (predicted state β_{t|t-1}, expanding z-score). Walk-forward OOS validation: Spearman IC, hit-rate, var-ratio≥0.20, Ljung-Box ≠ noise. COT Managed-Money overlay from POS-G8.

07 — Silver Fair Value · MDP (Kalman TVP / dual)

log(XAG) = α + βm·log(XAU) + βi·copper⊥gold · monetary + industrial channel · MDP z
Loading silver fair value — MFV_G8_state.json + MFV_G8_XAG.csv (repo)…
DUAL silver: monetary channel (time-varying beta on gold, shares the sovereign-disorder thermometer) + industrial channel (copper orthogonalized vs gold = HG⊥, pure industrial demand isolated from the safe-haven factor). The engine compares both models and lets the data decide: copper enters only if it improves the out-of-sample IC while respecting var-ratio≥0.20; otherwise silver reverts to the pure monetary model. Gold/silver ratio is informative, not a regressor (would be circular). Same Kalman TVP forward-filter, δ calibrated by walk-forward IC. Silver's MDP tends to a shorter half-life and larger amplitude than gold's, driven by its cyclical industrial component.
